Unit 1-12 Learning

Unit 1

At the beginning of the unit, I was new to approaches to software development. My understanding of software development, in general, was very shallow. The unit focused on the waterfall and agile approaches to software development. The agile approach simplifies the project's complexity, making it easier to understand and develop, and it also increases the efficiency of the team developing the software. Scalability is also easy with the agile approach. The waterfall approach is important in that the project's end goal can be determined early, and a clear structure of development is used. This unit has also equipped me with knowledge of Unified Modeling Language, which simplifies the system providing its blueprints. It is essential in software development because it helps to visualize the system and acts as a guide in constructing the system. From the examples and case scenarios learned during the unit study, I am in a position to design and draw UML diagrams for any complex project. Given any activity, I can decompose it into a set of tasks and represent it using a flowchart derived from a UML portfolio of modeling techniques. Developing secure software is the goal of every software development industry. In the unit, the standards to be used in the software development industry to ensure secure software are developed were discussed. Some of the measures are CERT, CVE, and IEC 62443. In addition to creating secure software, there is a need to make an effort to create risk awareness in an organization. Risk awareness involves educating the staff and software users of any potential risks that might face a system. By pointing out any potential risks, it is easier to mitigate them. During the study, I tackled several assignments to test my understanding of the general course. All the skills gained in class will be helpful to me if I decide to venture into software development. I would therefore recommend the unit be a necessity to all those wishing to be software developers.

Unit 2

I have gained a lot of knowledge from unit 2. Before starting the unit, I had very little knowledge of the integration of software security in the development process. During the study of the unit, we focused on the stages of agile SDLC. These stages include conception where the projects are prioritized, inception, which involves identifying team members, construction where team members develop the software, releasing the final project, production of the software, and retirement, which involves notifying the customer. At each of the mentioned stages, ensuring the software's security is essential as it prevents security breaches later. As a requirement completing the unit, I researched academic literature in software development that follows the agile process giving security a priority. From the research, it is clear that the software has to be broken down into small iterations for one to implement the agile approach, and each is of the same duration. The third outcome from the unit was the creation of UML flowcharts. I learned that it is necessary to analyze software before actually starting its implementation visually. The steps of the agile process listed above can be broken down further and represented in a flowchart to help understand the project before developing it. Lastly, emphasis was made on a range of ways in which software security can be a problem. Some of the issues listed were lack of information security knowledge, lack of security awareness, and the pressure of short iteration, making it hard to do necessary tests. I read on ISO/IEC standards which are very important in ensuring security. These are software standards internationally agreed upon by experts who describe the best way of software development. Currently, I am well familiar with the languages and concepts involved in these standards. There is a long list of these languages to mention two: ISO-639-1 and ISO 639-2.

Unit 8

In this unit I learnd that cryptography is a particularly interesting field because of the amount of work that is, by necessity, done in secret. The irony is that secrecy is not the key to cryptographic algorithm. Regardless of the mathematical theory behind an algorithm, the best algorithms are those that are well-known and well-documented because they are also well-tested and well-studied! In fact, time is the only true test of good cryptography; any cryptographic scheme that stays in use year after year, The strength of cryptography lies in the choice of the keys. The corollary to this is that organizations should run, not walk, away from any product that uses a proprietary cryptography scheme, ostensibly because the algorithm's secrecy is an advantage. The observation that a cryptosystem should be secure even if everything about the system — except the key — is known by your adversary has been a fundamental tenet of cryptography for well over 125 years.

Unit 10

In this unit, I have learned the evolution of system architectures. To be specific, I have a better understanding of how systems evolved from being distributed to microservices. This unit has broadened my mind about the technology of systems. As of now, I am in a better position to describe a variety of distributed systems. Some of these systems include networks, distributed database systems, and distributed real-time systems. Each of the stated systems evolved from monolithic to microservices. Choosing whether to use distributed systems has its strength and weaknesses. From the teachings in this unit, the resilience of distributed systems are that data can be shared easily between nodes, it is easy to add nodes to the system, and if one node fails, it does not lead to the failure of another node. The weaknesses of distributed systems are that loss of data during transmission is easier, providing adequate security systems is challenging also overloading often occurs in the network. Systems are always prone to attacks, and distributed systems are not an exception. Distributed systems are vulnerable to security attacks such as fraud vulnerabilities where one gains access to a system unlawfully. This can occur when confidential information is disclosed to unauthorized personnel. Vulnerability is the corruption of data when an unauthorized user may alter information from the system for his purposes. These systems are also prone to denial of service attacks, leading to the system failing to carry out its function. This threat is catastrophic and can lead to significant losses; hence it should be significantly considered. The unit focused on attacks on virtual systems. Ways in which the systems need to be protected from the attacks include: advancing malware protection which can be done by deploying threat protection on any virtual machine. Access control where administrators are required to apply proper control management on a virtual machine hosting servers, disaster recovery which involves integrating virtual machines such that data should be highly available and creating backup strategies for the data. Updating images of virtual systems regularly to ensure the security of data is also important. Generally, I feel this unit has improved my knowledge of computer systems. By digging deeper into the distributed systems, I can know when to and when not to recommend it to non-experts.
