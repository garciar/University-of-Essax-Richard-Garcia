Unit 9

This week I learnd the importance of log managment. Log management software is necessary in order to collect data, store it in a proper format for as long as needed, and help analyze issues and errors. Log management solutions allow system admins to generate their own rules and templates for generating alerts and look for patterns correlating with similar events. Extensive search options enable system admins to quickly sort, find, and compare past log activity with current activity. This is the first line of defence for the computer system. The biggest problem with logs is: nobody looks at them. From a security point of view, the purpose of a log is to act as a red flag when something bad is happening. Reviewing logs regularly could help identify malicious attacks on your system. Given the large of amount of log data generated by systems, it is impractical to review all of these logs manually each day. Log monitoring software takes care of that task by using rules to automate the review of these logs and only point out events that might represent problems or threats. Often this is done using real-time reporting systems that alert you via email or text when something suspicious is detected. On reflection the advantage of log management is to quickly nip attacks in the bud. One should setup a security strategy and make sure these steps are taken care of.

1. Decide how and when to generate logs

2. Secure your stored logs to make sure they aren’t maliciously altered by cybercriminals or accidentally altered by well-intentioned employees.

3. Assign an employee you trust to review logs daily.

4. Set up a team of people ready to review suspicious alerts.

Unit 10

In this unit I learned that in a security breach where one has an unauthorized person gets access to data, devices or networks investigation and troubleshooting the problem has to be done. A security breach often occurs when one bypasses security mechanisms. The first step is therefore the investigation phase where the damage is surveyed, the attacker identified and then the vulnerability is discovered. After the investigation is done troubleshooting the problem to identify possible solutions then starts. Additional damage has to be limited first to prevent unaffected areas from being affected, incoming traffic has then to be filtered, the system is then isolated for further protection measures. After successful troubleshooting, it’s important to keep a record of the findings for future reference as learning points.

Unit 11

In Unit 11 NNDC, I identified and analyzed security risks and vulnerabilities in IT Networks. Some of the risks facing these systems are malware infection, computer viruses and worms, phishing attacks, and distribution of denial of service attacks. When networks are exposed to these risks, there is a possibility of big data loss. To prevent the organizations from undergoing losses, appropriate methodologies and tools must be put in place to manage the issues. Regular full scans are essential to make sure the software is free from attacks. All systems need to be installed with anti-malware solutions to prevent malware and viruses infections. It is also necessary to be cautious when opening email links to reduce phishing attacks. To manage and audit risk and security issues, computer systems have to be designed to produce solutions to these issues. As part of the design, a security policy has to come first. Firewalls need to be introduced to the system, antiviruses installed and strong passwords put into use. Any new hires have to be screened first before being introduced to the system. In addition, anomalous activities need to be investigated on time. During the unit study, information from multiple sources was gathered to aid in the systematic analysis of security breaches and issues. From the resources compiled, to systematically analyze a security breach, the first step involves identifying the root cause of the issue. Once the cause is determined, measures to reduce the harm are quickly put in place to prevent further losses. After solving the issue, a record is kept to act as a future reference in case the security breach reoccurs. Lastly, the legal, social, ethical, and professional issues that information security professionals were discussed in the unit. Information security professionals most of the time have access to personal and confidential information pertaining to individuals and companies' networks and systems. It gives them the power to do anything with the information they have access to. IT associations and organizations had to come up and address the legal, ethical, social, and professional side of the job. The guidelines are essential because, during training, the specialists only focus on the technical parts of the job and forget that the skills can be misused.
